2. Neuroimaging Organization

General Neuro Rules
- Maintain standard neuroradiology order and cadence.
- Present clinically important acute findings first.
- Group related findings together.
- Avoid scattered or repetitive descriptions of the same abnormality.

CT Head / MRI Brain
Within FINDINGS, organize discussion in a clinically logical order:
- Hemorrhage / extra-axial collection / mass effect / midline shift
- Acute infarct or territorial gray-white loss
- Mass lesion or abnormal enhancement, if applicable
- Ventricles, sulci, cisterns, and hydrocephalus
- White matter disease / chronic infarcts / encephalomalacia
- Posterior fossa and brainstem
- Calvarium / skull base
- Orbits, paranasal sinuses, mastoids, and extracranial soft tissues

Use preferred terminology:
- “No acute intracranial hemorrhage.”
- “No large vascular territory loss of gray-white differentiation.”
- “No mass effect, midline shift, or hydrocephalus.”
- “Scattered/confluent periventricular and subcortical white matter hypoattenuation/signal abnormality, nonspecific but most commonly related to chronic microangiopathic change.”
- “Chronic encephalomalacia/gliosis” rather than vague “old insult” when appropriate.
- “Global cerebral volume loss” or “mild generalized parenchymal volume loss” rather than nonspecific “atrophy” when appropriate.

CTA Head/Neck / MRA Head/Neck
Within FINDINGS, organize vascular discussion in this order when applicable:
- Aortic arch and great vessel origins
- Right carotid system
- Left carotid system
- Vertebral arteries
- Intracranial anterior circulation
- Intracranial posterior circulation
- Aneurysm, vascular malformation, dissection, or occlusion
- Nonvascular neck / intracranial findings, if included

Use preferred terminology:
- “No large vessel occlusion or hemodynamically significant stenosis.”
- “No aneurysm or vascular malformation identified.”
- “Mild atherosclerotic plaque without hemodynamically significant stenosis.”
- “Patent cervical carotid and vertebral arteries without dissection or occlusion.”
- “Patent intracranial arterial vasculature without proximal large vessel occlusion.”

Avoid vague or nonstandard wording such as:
- “Blood vessels are normal.”
- “No blockage.”
- “Circle of Willis looks good.”

MRI Spine / CT Spine / XR Spine
Within FINDINGS, organize discussion in this order when applicable:
- Alignment and curvature
- Vertebral body heights
- Marrow signal / fracture / destructive osseous lesion
- Cord signal and morphology, if applicable
- Disc degeneration / endplate changes
- Canal stenosis
- Foraminal stenosis
- Facet arthropathy
- Paraspinal / prevertebral soft tissues

Use preferred terminology:
- “Straightening/reversal of the normal cervical lordosis.”
- “Trace grade 1 anterolisthesis/retrolisthesis.”
- “Vertebral body heights are maintained.”
- “No acute compression deformity.”
- “No focal cord signal abnormality.”
- “Multilevel spondylosis with disc space narrowing, endplate osteophytes, and facet arthropathy.”
- “Mild/moderate/severe spinal canal stenosis.”
- “Mild/moderate/severe neural foraminal stenosis.”
- “No prevertebral soft tissue swelling.”

For spine reports:
- Present alignment before degenerative disc/facet disease.
- Present canal stenosis before foraminal stenosis.
- Mention cord signal after canal stenosis when clinically relevant.
- Avoid saying “disc spaces are maintained” if disc space narrowing is described elsewhere.
- Avoid retaining “no subluxation” if listhesis is present; instead state the listhesis and whether secondary instability features are absent.

CT Face / Orbits / Sinus / Temporal Bone

- Nasal bones / facial bones / mandible / TMJs

- “No retrobulbar hematoma.”

- “Paranasal sinuses are clear.”
- “Mastoid air cells are clear.”
- “Odontogenic disease” rather than casual dental wording when appropriate.

6. Modality-Specific Cleanup Examples

If original wording says:
“No subluxation, significant grade listhesis or secondary features of instability.
Disc spaces and facet articulations are appropriately maintained.”

But findings include:

Multilevel cervical spondylosis with disc space narrowing and endplate osteophytes, severe at C5-C6 and C6-C7 and moderate at C4-C5.

Correct final wording:

Multilevel cervical spondylosis with disc space narrowing and endplate osteophytes, severe at C5-C6 and C6-C7 and moderate at C4-C5. Advanced multilevel facet arthropathy in the mid to lower cervical spine.”

Do not retain “disc spaces and facet articulations are appropriately maintained” when degenerative disc disease or facet arthropathy is present.
